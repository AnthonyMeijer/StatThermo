I Properties of logarithms

ln⁡(Z*W)=ln⁡Z+ln⁡W (1)
ln⁡(Z/W)=ln⁡Z−ln⁡W (2)
ln⁡(ZW)=W⁢ln⁡Z (3)

Thus e.g. (for indistinguishable particles),

ln⁡Q=ln⁡qN/N!=ln⁡qN−ln⁡N!=N⁢ln⁡q−ln⁡N! (4)

II Differentiation

  • •

    Sum-rule:

    ∂∂s⁢[h⁢(s,t)+g⁢(s,t)]=∂h⁢(s,t)∂s+∂g⁢(s,t)∂s (5)
  • •

    Product-rule:

    ∂∂s⁢[h⁢(s,t)*g⁢(s,t)]=g⁢(s,t)*∂h⁢(s,t)∂s+h⁢(s,t)*∂g⁢(s,t)∂s (6)
  • •

    Chain-rule:

    ∂∂s⁢[h⁢(g⁢(s,t))]=∂h∂s*∂g⁢(s,t)∂s (7)

Thus, e.g.

∂∂V⁢ln⁡Q=1Q⁢∂Q∂V (8)

III Integration by parts

  • •

    If the integrand is a product of two separate terms, both of which depend on the integration variable, then, if you can view the integrand as u⁢(x)*d⁢v⁢(x)d⁢x, the integral can possibly (not always) be solved using integration by parts. In general the procedure is as follows:

    ∫u⁢(x)×d⁢v⁢(x)d⁢x⁢𝑑x=[u×v]−∫d⁢u⁢(x)d⁢x×v⁢(x)⁢𝑑x (9)

    Example: You want to integrate x*cos⁡(x). Then u⁢(x)=x and v⁢(x)=sin⁡(x) [because d⁢v⁢(x)d⁢x would be cos⁡(x)]. So, the integral becomes:

    ∫x⁢cos⁡(x)⁢𝑑x=[x⁢sin⁡(x)]−∫1×sin⁡(x)⁢𝑑x=x⁢sin⁡(x)+c⁢o⁢s⁢(x)+C (10)

    See Wikipedia or Mathworld for more details.

    Consider the relationship between specific heat capacity at constant volume and entropy (Remember that: U=U⁢(0)−N⁢(∂ln⁡q∂β)V=U⁢(0)−(∂ln⁡Q∂β)V=U⁢(0)+k⁢T2⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V.

    = 1T⁢∂∂T⁢[k⁢T2⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V]⁢d⁢T (18)
    =product⁢rule 1T⁢[2⁢k⁢T⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V+k⁢T2⁢(∂2ln⁡Q∂T2)V]⁢d⁢T (19)
    = [2⁢k⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V+k⁢T⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V]⁢d⁢T (20)

    Then,

    S=∫𝑑S=∫2⁢k⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V⁢𝑑T+∫k⁢T⁢(∂2ln⁡Q∂T2)V⁢𝑑T (21)

    The first integral can be done straight forwardly and gives 2⁢k⁢ln⁡Q+C, but the second one needs to be done using integration by parts. Here, the obvious choice to take is to assign

    u⁢(T)=T⁢and⁢∂v⁢(T)∂T=(∂2ln⁡Q∂T2)V (22)

    giving:

    ∫k⁢T⁢(∂2ln⁡Q∂T2)V⁢𝑑T=k⁢T⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V−∫k⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V⁢𝑑T+C′ (23)

    whereby the remaining integral on the right hand side of Eq. (23) gives k⁢ln⁡Q+C′′. Thus, taking all terms together and taking integration constants together C, C′, and C′′ into S⁢(0) gives:

    S=∫𝑑S=2⁢k⁢ln⁡Q+k⁢T⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V−k⁢ln⁡Q=k⁢ln⁡Q+k⁢T⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V+S⁢(0) (24)

    which is Eq. 7.7 in the Maczek book. Taking [A−A⁢(0)]=[U−U⁢(0)]−T⁢[S−S⁢(0)] then leads to the expression for the Helmholtz energy as

    A−A⁢(0)=k⁢T2⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V−k⁢T⁢ln⁡Q−k⁢T2⁢(∂ln⁡Q∂T)V=−k⁢T⁢ln⁡Q (25)

    Setting S⁢(0) to zero as per the third law of thermodynamics then also means that U⁢(0) and A⁢(0) need to be the same.
